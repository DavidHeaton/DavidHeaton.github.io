Attacking Tiips

Did I Hit?

Roll a d20: To determine if you hit the target, roll a d20 and add your attack modifier (based on Strength or Dexterity, depending on the weapon). If you're proficient with the weapon, add your proficiency bonus.

Compare the Result to the Target's Armor Class (AC): If your roll meets or exceeds the AC, you hit.

How Much Damage?

If you hit, roll the weapon's damage dice and add any relevant modifiers. For example, if your weapon deals 1d6 damage and your Dexterity modifier is +5, you add +5 to the roll.

Sneak Attacks

If you have advantage on the attack or an ally is within 5 feet of the target, you can apply your Sneak Attack damage (3d6 at Level 5). This is added to the regular weapon damage.

Uncanny Dodge

If you are hit by an attack that you can see, you can use your reaction to halve the damage.

Crits

If you roll a natural 20 on the attack roll, you score a critical hit. When you score a critical hit, roll the damage dice twice and then add any modifiers.

Two-Weapon Fighting

If you're wielding two light weapons, you can use your bonus action to make an attack with your off-hand weapon. You don’t add your modifier to the off-hand weapon damage, except for your Dexterity modifier.

Opportunity Attacks

When a hostile creature you can see moves out of your reach, you can use your reaction to make an opportunity attack against that creature.

Ranged Attacks

When making a ranged attack, make sure you're within the weapon's range (normal or long range) and that there is nothing obstructing your line of sight.
